## Topic

Laid-back Chengdu teahouse culture showcasing He Ming Tea House, the city's oldest and most famous teahouse established in 1923. This content targets overseas audiences interested in authentic Chinese cultural experiences, traditional tea culture, and Chengdu's distinctive lifestyle. The teahouse combines century-old heritage, premium tea selections, traditional performances, and vibrant local atmosphere to demonstrate why Chengdu has more teahouses than other Chinese cities.

## Top Hooks

- It was built 100 years ago.
- Well, right in the park, the most famous tea house in Chengdu is here.
- and directly translates into English as snowflakes floating in a green lake.
- So actually, this teahouse is the oldest teahouse in Chengdu.

## Selling Points

- **Historic Teahouse Heritage**: Visit the most famous tea house in Chengdu, established in 1923 with nearly 100 years of history
- **Premium Tea Selection**: Try jasmine tea poetically named 'snowflakes floating in a green lake'
- **Top-Tier Tea Experience**: Sample Meng Ding yellow tea, ranked within the top ten teas in China
- **Kung Fu Performance**: Witness traditional kung fu tea performance with long-bead teapot demonstrations
- **Secret Tea Language**: Discover the secret language of tea lid positioning used for service signals
- **Authentic Local Culture**: Join locals from 7 AM for morning tea in a vibrant, authentically busy atmosphere
- **Teahouse Capital**: Explore tea culture in a city with more teahouses than other cities in China
- **Cross-Generational Appeal**: See a surprising mix of young and old people embracing traditional tea culture

## Themes

**Historic Teahouse Heritage**: Experience the oldest and most famous teahouse in Chengdu, established in 1923 with nearly a century of history.

**Premium Tea Selection**: Savor premium teas including Meng Ding yellow tea ranked in China's top ten and poetically-named jasmine tea.

**Tea Etiquette & Customs**: Master traditional tea drinking etiquette including gender-specific techniques and the secret language of tea lid positioning.

**Kung Fu Tea Performance**: Watch skilled performers demonstrate traditional kung fu tea techniques using long-bead teapots with impressive balance and precision.

**Chengdu Tea Culture & Atmosphere**: Immerse yourself in Chengdu's vibrant tea culture where locals of all ages gather from early morning in the city's most teahouse-rich destination.

## Recommendations

1. Lead with the century-old heritage angle and People's Park location to establish authenticity and accessibility for first-time visitors to Chengdu.
2. Feature the poetic tea names and premium selections to appeal to culture-curious travelers seeking unique experiences beyond tourist attractions.
3. Demonstrate the tea lid positioning signals and gender-specific drinking etiquette as shareable "insider knowledge" that encourages engagement.
4. Capture the early morning atmosphere with young locals to counter stereotypes about traditional tea culture being only for older generations.
5. Showcase the kung fu tea performance as visual spectacle content while positioning the teahouse as a living cultural institution rather than a tourist performance venue.
