## Topic

First-time visitor guide to Shanghai covering essential setup requirements for navigating China's digital-first infrastructure, including payment systems, connectivity, transportation, and legal compliance.

## Top Hooks

- "If you're traveling to China for the first time, there are a few things you need to know"
- "I've been living in Shanghai for nearly 20 years"
- "not every hotel is allowed to host foreign nationals"
- "Cash is seldom used, especially in the big cities"
- "your phone doesn't work"

## Selling Points

- **Expert guidance from someone with nearly 20 years of living in Shanghai** establishing credibility and insider knowledge for overcoming common first-timer obstacles
- **Complete walkthrough of linking foreign credit cards to Alipay and WeChat** for QR code payments throughout China, solving the cashless payment barrier
- **Step-by-step demonstrations of getting free airport Wi-Fi** without a local phone number using passport kiosks
- **Convenient help desks at Pudong Airport** for local SIM cards and digital payment setup with Alipay
- **Demonstrated method for buying subway tickets** using Alipay QR codes with English language option
- **Detailed guidance on which hotels are legally allowed to host foreign nationals** and automatic police registration
- **Built-in translation feature in Alipay** for ordering food from Chinese-only menus on delivery apps
- **Practical advice for passport holders buying high-speed train tickets** at staffed windows with English-speaking attendants

## Themes

**Digital Payment Systems** – Shows how to navigate China's cashless society by setting up Alipay and WeChat for QR code payments with foreign credit cards.

**Connectivity & Communication** – Demonstrates solutions for phone connectivity and language barriers including airport Wi-Fi setup and translation tools.

**Transportation Navigation** – Guides travelers through Shanghai's airports, reliable metro system, and high-speed train ticket purchasing for passport holders.

**Accommodation & Legal Requirements** – Explains critical legal requirements including hotel restrictions for foreigners and mandatory police registration within 24 hours.

**Expert Credibility & Demonstrations** – Provides trustworthy first-time travel advice backed by 20 years of Shanghai experience and real on-location demonstrations.

## Recommendations

1. Lead with the dual pain points of non-functional phones and cashless society, then immediately position yourself as the solution provider with specific, demonstrable fixes filmed on-location.

2. Structure content as a chronological arrival checklist starting at airport Wi-Fi access, then SIM cards, digital payment setup, hotel registration, and transportation—mirroring the actual first-timer journey.

3. Film all critical steps as real-time demonstrations at actual locations (Pudong Airport kiosks, metro stations, train terminals) rather than explanatory talking-head segments to build trust and replicability.

4. Emphasize the legal compliance angle (hotel restrictions, 24-hour police registration) early to establish this as essential information tourists won't find in typical travel guides.

5. Package the Alipay setup as the master key that unlocks multiple systems—subway tickets, food delivery with translation, and general purchases—to maximize perceived value of learning this single platform.
