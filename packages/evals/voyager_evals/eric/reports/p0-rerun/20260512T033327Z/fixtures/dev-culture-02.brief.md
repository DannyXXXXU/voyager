## Topic

Chengdu's century-old He Ming Tea House showcases the city's legendary laid-back lifestyle through traditional tea culture, premium Chinese teas, and authentic rituals that attract both locals and travelers seeking an immersive cultural experience.

## Top Hooks

- It was built 100 years ago.
- snowflakes floating in a green lake
- They will think you're not going to pay for the bill.
- within the top ten teas in China
- Chengdu people have such a laid back lifestyle.

## Selling Points

- **He Ming Tea House established in 1923** — Nearly a century of continuous tea service provides historical credibility and authentic heritage appeal.
- **Jasmine tea 'snowflakes floating in a green lake'** — The poetic Chinese name (Bi Tan Piao Xue) creates visual intrigue and cultural depth.
- **Meng Ding yellow tea (top ten teas in China)** — Premium tea ranking signals quality and exclusivity for discerning travelers.
- **Kung fu tea performance with long-bead teapot** — Visually dynamic performances offer shareable content moments.
- **Historic tea lid signaling system for service requests** — Unique etiquette rules create curiosity and interactive learning opportunities.
- **Traditional gender-specific tea drinking etiquette** — Cultural contrast points engage audiences with unexpected customs.
- **Tea culture starting at 7am in People's Park** — Early morning vibrancy demonstrates tea culture as a living daily practice, not tourist performance.
- **Chengdu has more teahouses than other cities in China** — Superlative positioning establishes Chengdu as the definitive tea culture destination.

## Themes

**Century-Old Tea House Heritage** — He Ming Tea House has been serving tea for nearly 100 years since its establishment in 1923.

**Premium Chinese Tea Selection** — The tea house offers prestigious teas including jasmine 'snowflakes floating in a green lake' and Meng Ding yellow tea, ranked among China's top ten.

**Traditional Tea Etiquette & Performance** — Guests experience authentic traditions including kung fu tea performances, gender-specific drinking styles, and a historic lid-signaling system for service.

**Chengdu's Vibrant Tea Culture** — Chengdu's laid-back lifestyle centers on tea culture with more teahouses than any Chinese city, attracting all ages from early morning onward.

## Recommendations

1. Lead with mysterious etiquette hooks like the tea lid signaling system to create immediate curiosity, then reveal the cultural meaning as payoff.
2. Emphasize the cross-generational appeal by showcasing young people participating in traditional tea culture to counter "old-fashioned" perceptions.
3. Use the poetic tea names and kung fu performances as visual anchors for short-form content while reserving the century-old heritage story for longer narrative formats.
4. Position Chengdu as the ultimate slow-living destination by contrasting the 7am tea culture with rushed Western morning routines.
5. Create comparison content highlighting Chengdu's tea house density versus other Chinese cities to establish authoritative positioning.
