## Topic

Guide for first-timers to Shanghai covering essential setup, payments, accommodation, and navigation for international travelers unfamiliar with China's digital infrastructure and regulatory requirements.

## Top Hooks

- If you're traveling to China for the first time, there are a few things you need to know
- I've been living in Shanghai for nearly 20 years, and in this video, I'll be demonstrating some of the things you can do to make your stay in the city a lot more convenient
- Booking a hotel in China can be a little tricky, as not every hotel is allowed to host foreign nationals
- Paying for things in China is a little bit different than other countries
- Cash is seldom used, especially in the big cities

## Themes

**Digital Payment & App Setup** – Comprehensive guide to setting up Alipay and WeChat with international credit cards for cashless payments throughout China, including QR code metro access and airport help desk.

**Accommodation & Registration Requirements** – Critical information on booking hotels that accept foreign nationals and completing mandatory police registration within 24 hours of arrival in Shanghai.

**Transportation & Ticketing** – Practical advice for foreign passport holders to purchase high-speed train tickets through human-staffed windows instead of automated kiosks.

**Language Solutions & Connectivity** – Essential language barrier solutions including airport Wi-Fi setup, Alipay's translation feature for food delivery, and phone camera translation for restaurant menus.

**Expert Local Guidance & Resources** – Insider knowledge from 20 years of Shanghai living, including official government resources and WeChat reservation methods for tourist attractions requiring advance booking.

1. Lead with credibility by establishing long-term resident expertise in the first 10 seconds to build trust with first-time travelers facing unfamiliar systems.

3. Structure content around arrival order: airport Wi-Fi first, then payment apps, hotel check-in with registration requirements, and finally ongoing navigation tools.
4. Include specific location markers and visual demonstrations for help desks and service counters to reduce friction when viewers arrive on the ground.
